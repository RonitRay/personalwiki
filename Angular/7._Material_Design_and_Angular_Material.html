7. Material Design and Angular Material

Created Saturday 10 July 2021

Material Design is a design language developed by Google in 2014. Expanding on the "cards" that debuted in Google Now, Material Design uses more grid-based layouts, responsive animations and transitions, padding, and depth effects such as lighting and shadows.

Material design is a set of aesthetic and functional guidelines that developers can follow to make their apps beautiful and consistent.

[Material Design](https://material.io/design)

[Material Design](https://material.io/components)

However, translating design (UI/UX) guidelines to actual code is very difficult for frontend developers.

This is why the team at Google has provided Angular Material- a library of reusable modules and components that can be integrated swiftly with your Angular project to give it material design-inspired look throughout.

[Angular Material](https://material.angular.io/)

Get started quickly:

[Angular Material](https://material.angular.io/guide/getting-started)

Check out the various components available:

[Angular Material](https://material.angular.io/components/categories)

You can also use Schematics or pre-built layouts to get started very quickly.

[Angular Material](https://material.angular.io/guide/schematics)

Follow the tutorial from Codevolution on YouTube for any issues:

[Angular Material Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLC3y8-rFHvwilEuCqFGTL5Gt5U6deIrsU)
