Get Headset working through TRRS combi-jack

Created Wednesday 14 July 2021

This is stupid and maybe distro and device-specific, but it's an issue with my laptop so I'm keeping it here.

So basically headsets of old came with TRS 3-ring connectors, and they had separate connectors for headphone and mic. Most desktops still have these two ports separated.

However, newer earphones with mics as well as new headsets come with a single TRRS 4 ring plug. So to use them with 2-port sound cards, we need a 1:2 female to male splitter.

https://www.amazon.in/gp/product/B08BCKN299/ref=ppx_yo_dt_b_search_asin_title?ie=UTF8&psc=1

But if you have a laptop with a single TRRS jack and a headset with a single TRRS plug, it seems to be a toss-up whether it will work or not. I spent the longest time thinking it just didn't support mics and I would only have to use USB mics. Turns out a single thingamajiggy in linux just "activates" an existing inbuilt functionality.

Why is alsa. Why is Pulseaudio.

Anyway: 

sudo vi /etc/modprobe.d/alsa-base.conf


options snd-hda-intel model=,dell-headset-multi
options snd-hda-intel position fix=1

And reboot the machine. It should trigger the internal mechanism that asks whether you plugged in headphones, a mic, or a combi-headset when you do now.
